# MIS 311 - Data Analysis Project

## Project Title
Most Visited Countries Analysis

## Project Description

This project analyses international tourist arrivals across different countries using descriptive statistics and data visualisation techniques.

### 1. Data Overview

I selected the dataset “Most Visited Countries”. It contains tourism arrival data from different countries around the world. The dataset includes actual international tourist arrivals in 2022 and 2023, predicted tourist arrivals for 2024, and tourism statistics from the World Bank. The dataset contains 207 rows (including the header row) and 6 columns. The 6 rcolums represent 6 variables: Country, Predicted tourist arrivals in 2024, Tourist arrivals in 2023, Tourist arrivals in 2022, World Bank tourist arrival data and World Bank year.
This dataset shows which countries are experiencing tourism growth each year. It provides a basis for analyzing changes in international tourism patterns over time.
### 2. Data Cleaning

The dataset was checked for missing values using Excel.
Using the “Go to Special” function, and remove entire blank rows. I identified a total of 188 rows that have missing values. These rows were removed to improve the accuracy and reliability of the data for analysis.
After deleting the missing value, I used Excel's "Remove Duplicates" feature to identify the duplicate information. There were no duplicate rows found in the dataset.
As a result, we have 17 rows left (exclude the header row).

Insight 1

The box plot shows that the mean of tourist arrivals increased from 31.41 million in 2022 to 38.49 million in 2023 and remained high in the 2024 forecast at 38.32 million. Simultaneously, the maximum value also increased from 79.4 million to 100 million in 2023. This indicates that the international tourism industry has recovered strongly and continues to maintain a stable growth trend.

Insight 2

The median of tourists in all three years was lower than the mean and the range in 3 years is significantly high, they are all above 65 million. It suggests that a few countries with very high tourist numbers artificially inflated the overall average of the data. This shows that the distribution of international tourists is uneven, with most being concentrated in popular destinations around the world.
